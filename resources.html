Resources for Your Success

Tips and tools to help you succeed with treatment.

CPAP Success Tips

Choose the right mask and make sure it fits well. Start by wearing your CPAP while reading or watching TV to get used to the sensation. Use a humidifier to reduce dryness. Keep equipment clean and track your usage. Stay patient—most people need a few weeks to adapt.

Understanding Allergies and Nasal Congestion

Nasal obstruction makes mouth breathing more likely and worsens snoring and OSA. Treating congestion with nasal sprays, saline rinses, antihistamines, or allergy therapy can make a big difference in comfort and response to CPAP.

Weight Loss and Sleep Apnea

Weight around the neck and upper airway can worsen sleep-disordered breathing. A small amount of weight loss can improve AHI (apnea-hypopnea index) and sometimes eliminate the need for CPAP. We offer structured nutrition support and weight loss medications when appropriate.

Your Role in Treatment

Stay consistent with treatment, communicate challenges, and be open to trying alternative therapies if needed. Treating sleep apnea is a process—we’ll support you every step of the way to make sure you’re getting results and feeling better.
