Understanding Sleep-Disordered Breathing (SDB)

Learn about the spectrum of conditions that affect your breathing during sleep and how they impact health.

Types of SDB

  • Primary Snoring: Noisy breathing during sleep without oxygen drops or awakenings.
  • Upper Airway Resistance Syndrome (UARS): Subtle airway narrowing causing sleep disruption.
  • Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA): Repeated pauses in breathing due to blocked airway.

Common Symptoms

  • Loud snoring
  • Excessive daytime fatigue
  • Morning headaches
  • Waking up gasping or choking
  • Difficulty concentrating

Why It Matters

Untreated SDB can lead to high blood pressure, heart disease, stroke, and decreased quality of life. Treating it early improves sleep and long-term health.
