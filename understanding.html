What is Sleep-Disordered Breathing?

Sleep-disordered breathing (SDB) refers to a group of conditions that limit airflow during sleep. These range from snoring to serious issues like obstructive sleep apnea (OSA).

Types of SDB

  • Primary Snoring: Noisy breathing during sleep without oxygen drops or awakenings.
  • Upper Airway Resistance Syndrome (UARS): Subtle narrowing causing sleep disruption.
  • Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA): Repeated pauses or shallow breathing due to blocked airway.

Common Symptoms

  • Loud snoring
  • Excessive daytime fatigue
  • Morning headaches
  • Waking up gasping or choking
  • Difficulty concentrating

Why It Matters

Untreated SDB can lead to high blood pressure, heart disease, stroke, and decreased quality of life. Treating it early improves sleep and long-term health.
