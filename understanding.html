Understanding Sleep-Disordered Breathing

Learn about how sleep-disordered breathing affects your health and what symptoms to watch for.

Types of Sleep-Disordered Breathing

Primary Snoring: Noisy breathing during sleep caused by vibration of tissues in the throat.

Upper Airway Resistance Syndrome (UARS): Mild narrowing of the airway causing sleep disruption without oxygen drops.

Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA): Repeated full or partial airway blockages, leading to reduced oxygen levels and measured using the Apnea-Hypopnea Index (AHI).

Common Symptoms

  • Loud, chronic snoring
  • Morning headaches
  • Waking up gasping or choking
  • Daytime fatigue
