Understanding Sleep-Disordered Breathing

Learn about how sleep-disordered breathing affects your health and what symptoms to watch for.

Types of SDB

  • Primary Snoring: Noisy breathing during sleep without oxygen drops or awakenings.
  • UARS: Subtle narrowing causing sleep disruption and fatigue.
  • OSA: Repeated pauses in breathing due to blocked airway.

Common Symptoms

  • Loud snoring
  • Excessive daytime fatigue
  • Morning headaches
  • Waking up gasping or choking
  • Memory or focus issues

Why It Matters

Untreated SDB can lead to high blood pressure, heart disease, stroke, and decreased quality of life. Early diagnosis and treatment can improve your sleep and health.
