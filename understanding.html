What is Sleep-Disordered Breathing?

SDB includes a spectrum of conditions characterized by abnormal breathing patterns during sleep. These can interrupt sleep cycles, lower oxygen levels, and strain the body over time.

Types of SDB

Primary Snoring: Benign but disruptive, may indicate airflow issues.

Upper Airway Resistance Syndrome (UARS): Narrowed airway causing effortful breathing and fatigue.

Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA): Repeated airway blockage with oxygen drops and frequent arousals.

Symptoms & Health Risks

  • Loud snoring
  • Daytime fatigue
  • Morning headaches
  • Memory issues
  • Increased risk of hypertension, heart disease, and diabetes
