Melatonin as a Sleep Aid

Understanding how melatonin works and whether it's right for you.

Melatonin is a natural hormone produced by your brain in response to darkness. It helps regulate your sleep-wake cycle and signals to your body that it’s time to rest. Because of this role, melatonin supplements are commonly used to help people fall asleep more easily.

How It Works

  • Triggers sleep: Helps shift your internal clock, especially helpful for jet lag or shift work.
  • Does not sedate: Melatonin is not a sedative — it signals your body to start winding down rather than forcing sleep.
  • Best taken 30–60 minutes before bed: Timing is important for effectiveness.

Things to Know

  • Lower doses (0.3–1 mg) may be just as effective as higher ones.
  • Quality can vary — melatonin is not tightly regulated in supplements.
  • May cause vivid dreams or grogginess in some people.

Bottom Line

Melatonin may be a safe, short-term option for certain sleep issues. If you're considering it as part of your routine, it’s best to talk with your healthcare provider to ensure it fits your overall sleep treatment plan.
