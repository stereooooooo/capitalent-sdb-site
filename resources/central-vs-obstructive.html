Central vs. Obstructive Sleep Apnea

What’s the difference between these two types of sleep apnea?

Sleep apnea happens when your breathing is interrupted during sleep. There are two main types: obstructive sleep apnea (OSA) and central sleep apnea (CSA). While both involve pauses in breathing, the causes — and treatments — are different.

Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA)

  • Most common type of sleep apnea
  • Caused by physical blockage of the airway (e.g., soft palate or tongue collapse)
  • Often associated with snoring, choking, or gasping during sleep
  • Usually treated with CPAP, oral appliances, or surgery

Central Sleep Apnea (CSA)

  • Less common and often more complex
  • Happens when the brain doesn’t send the right signals to the breathing muscles
  • Not usually linked to snoring
  • May be related to heart failure, certain medications, or altitude
  • Treatment may include specialized breathing devices like ASV

Bottom Line

It’s important to know which type of sleep apnea you have so you can get the right treatment. This is one of the reasons a sleep study is so important — it helps identify whether breathing pauses are due to obstruction or a brain signaling issue.
