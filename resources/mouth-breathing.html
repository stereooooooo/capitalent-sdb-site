How Mouth Breathing Affects Sleep

Why nasal breathing is healthier — and how to improve it.

Mouth breathing during sleep is more common than many people realize. While breathing through your nose is optimal, some people rely on their mouth due to congestion, allergies, or habit — and that can affect your sleep.

Why Nasal Breathing Is Better

  • Filters and humidifies air: Your nose traps allergens and adds moisture to the air you breathe.
  • Improves airflow pressure: Nasal breathing supports proper pressure in the upper airway.
  • Promotes nitric oxide production: Which helps oxygen absorption and blood flow.

Risks of Mouth Breathing at Night

  • Dry mouth, sore throat, or dental issues
  • More likely to snore or develop obstructive sleep apnea
  • Poor sleep quality and fragmented sleep

What You Can Do

Addressing the root cause is key. Treatments might include allergy care, nasal sprays, breathing exercises, or in some cases nasal surgery. Chin straps or mouth tape may be recommended in some situations — but always consult a professional first.
