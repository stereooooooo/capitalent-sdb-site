Insomnia and Sleep Apnea

When trouble falling asleep and trouble staying asleep overlap.

Insomnia and sleep apnea are two of the most common sleep problems. Many people actually experience both at the same time. This is called COMISA — co-morbid insomnia and sleep apnea — and it can be tricky to manage.

How They Interact

  • Sleep apnea can cause frequent awakenings that make it hard to stay asleep
  • Insomnia can cause stress and anxiety that make it hard to fall asleep
  • The combination leads to poor sleep quality and daytime fatigue

Tips That May Help

  • Use CPAP consistently to reduce awakenings from sleep apnea
  • Practice a consistent bedtime routine and wind-down period
  • Avoid naps, caffeine late in the day, and phone use at night
  • Cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT-I) is often helpful for insomnia

Bottom Line

If you’re being treated for sleep apnea but still feel tired, insomnia could be part of the picture. We can help identify the cause and find a treatment approach that fits your needs.
