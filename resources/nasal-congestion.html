Nasal Congestion at Night

Why your nose feels blocked — and how it affects your sleep.

Struggling to breathe through your nose at night can seriously impact your sleep quality. Whether it's allergies, a deviated septum, or sinus inflammation, nighttime congestion makes it harder to rest.

Common Causes of Nasal Blockage

  • Seasonal or year-round allergies
  • Deviated nasal septum or enlarged turbinates
  • Sinus infections or chronic sinusitis
  • Nasal valve collapse

Impact on Sleep

  • Increases mouth breathing and snoring
  • Reduces oxygen flow and disrupts sleep cycles
  • Makes CPAP therapy less comfortable and less effective

What Can Help

We offer treatments including nasal sprays, allergy therapy, VivAer® for nasal valve collapse, and sinus surgery if needed. A nasal endoscopy or CT scan can help us determine the cause of your congestion.

Bottom Line

You don’t have to settle for blocked breathing at night. Treatment can improve airflow — and your quality of sleep.
