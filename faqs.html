Frequently Asked Questions

Your questions answered about sleep-disordered breathing, diagnosis, and treatment.

SDB refers to a group of conditions — including snoring, upper airway resistance syndrome (UARS), and obstructive sleep apnea (OSA) — in which breathing is disrupted during sleep. It can affect sleep quality and overall health.

Common signs include loud snoring, gasping or choking at night, waking unrefreshed, excessive daytime sleepiness, brain fog, irritability, and difficulty concentrating.

The Inspire implant is appropriate for certain patients with moderate to severe obstructive sleep apnea who are unable to tolerate CPAP. You must have a body mass index (BMI) under a certain threshold and undergo a brief airway evaluation called Drug-Induced Sleep Endoscopy (DISE). Our team can help assess whether you’re a candidate based on your anatomy and sleep study results.

Our office will get insurance approval. Our sleep study coordinator will then contact you to let you know when the device is either ready to be picked up from our office or mailed to you. You typically only need to use the home sleep study device for one night and then return it the next day. We will then either call you to notify you of your results and discuss next steps or will ask you to return to the office to discuss the results and treatment plan in person.
