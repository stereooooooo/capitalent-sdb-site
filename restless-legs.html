Restless Legs Syndrome and Sleep

Why your legs won’t sit still — and how it affects your rest.

Restless Legs Syndrome (RLS) is a condition where your legs feel uncomfortable or jittery, especially at night. It often creates an irresistible urge to move your legs, which can make falling or staying asleep difficult.

What Causes It?

  • Exact cause isn’t fully understood but may involve low dopamine levels in the brain
  • More common in women and older adults
  • Can be linked to low iron levels, kidney disease, or pregnancy
  • May run in families

How It Affects Sleep

  • Symptoms usually get worse at night
  • Moving the legs can help temporarily but disrupts sleep cycles
  • May lead to insomnia, daytime sleepiness, or trouble staying asleep

What Can Help

Treatment may include iron supplements, exercise, avoiding caffeine/alcohol, or medications that affect dopamine or nerve pathways.

Bottom Line

If you feel like your legs are keeping you up at night, talk to your doctor. Managing RLS can make a big difference in how well you sleep.
